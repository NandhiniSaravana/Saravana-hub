ISO 9001-2018

ISO 9001 is a globally recognized quality management system (QMS) standard developed by the International Organization for Standardization (ISO). It provides a framework for businesses to ensure consistent product and service quality, customer satisfaction, and continuous improvement.

ISO 9001 has its roots in military and industrial quality standards and has evolved to become the world’s most widely used quality management standard.

Current Version – ISO 9001:2015

  • Adopted a risk-based approach to quality management.
  • Greater emphasis on leadership, risk management, and performance evaluation.
  • Made applicable to all industries, not just manufacturing.

BRCGS

BRCGS (British Retail Consortium Global Standards) is a globally recognized food safety, packaging, and consumer product certification system. It was developed by the British Retail Consortium (BRC) to establish consistent quality, safety, and operational standards in the food, packaging, and retail industries.

BRCGS is now one of the most widely used supplier certification programs, ensuring compliance with global safety and quality regulations.

Present – BRCGS as a Global Benchmark for Quality & Safety

  • Over 30,000 sites across 130+ countries are now BRCGS certified.
  • The standard continues to evolve to include food fraud prevention, allergen control, and environmental impact assessments.

SQP

The Supplier Qualification Program (SQP) is a global supplier audit framework designed to evaluate and ensure product quality, safety, and ethical manufacturing. SQP was developed to provide a comprehensive supplier assessment, focusing on risk management, compliance, and continuous improvement.

Originally designed for major retailers and brands, SQP is now used across multiple industries, including packaging, printing, textiles, and consumer goods manufacturing.

Present – SQP as a Key Component of Global Sourcing Strategies

  • Now widely used by retailers, brands, and regulatory bodies to prequalify and monitor suppliers.
  • A critical requirement for suppliers aiming to do business with large multinational companies.

FCCA (Walmart Buyer)

The Factory Capability & Capacity Assessment (FCCA) is a Walmart supplier audit designed to ensure that factories producing Walmart products have the necessary capabilities, quality controls, and production capacity to meet Walmart’s standards.

FCCA focuses on factory infrastructure, production processes, quality management, and risk mitigation to ensure consistent product quality and on-time delivery.

Present – FCCA as a Global Standard for Walmart Suppliers

  • FCCA is now a mandatory pre-qualification for factories supplying Walmart globally.
  • It ensures efficiency, compliance, and continuous improvement in manufacturing.

JQMP Training (Walmart)

The Junior Qualification Management Program (JQMP) is a Walmart initiative designed to ensure that suppliers meet Walmart’s strict quality and compliance standards. This program focuses on manufacturing, product safety, ethical sourcing, and continuous improvement for Walmart’s suppliers, especially in printing, packaging, textiles, and general merchandise.

2020s – Digital Transformation & Supplier Audits

  • Walmart introduced digital tools and data analytics to track supplier performance, defect rates, and compliance metrics.
  • JQMP became a key requirement for new suppliers entering Walmart’s supply chain, ensuring quality, efficiency, and ethical production.

Present – Global Expansion & Continuous Improvement

  • JQMP is now part of Walmart’s broader supplier qualification programs, ensuring all products meet high safety, quality, and ethical standards.

Officeworks (QMS)

Officeworks is Australia’s leading retailer for office supplies, technology, furniture, and educational products. Since its founding, the company has grown into a major one-stop destination for businesses, students, and professionals, offering stationery, printing, and technology solutions both in-store and online.

2020s – Growth in E-commerce & Sustainable Business Practices

  • Online sales surged, making Officeworks one of Australia’s top e-commerce retailers.
  • Launched "Restoring Australia", a tree-planting initiative to offset paper sales.
  • Focused on reducing plastic waste, increasing energy efficiency, and supporting local businesses.

GRS

The Global Recycled Standard (GRS) was developed to promote the use of recycled materials in textiles, packaging, and manufacturing while ensuring environmental and social responsibility throughout the supply chain. It helps companies verify recycled content, responsible production practices, and chemical restrictions in their products.

2021–Present – Digital Advancements & Circular Economy

  • GRS certification incorporated blockchain and digital tracking for better material traceability.
  • The standard became a key part of corpor ate sustainability goals and ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) compliance.
  • Governments and industries adopted recycling mandates and circular economy policies to support sustainable manufacturing.

FSC

The Forest Stewardship Council (FSC) was established to promote responsible forest management worldwide. It ensures that forests are managed sustainably, balancing environmental, social, and economic benefits. FSC certification is now a global standard for responsible forestry, widely used in the paper, packaging, and wood industries.

Present – FSC as a Global Leader in Sustainable Forestry

  • Over 230 million hectares of forest are FSC-certified across 90+ countries.
  • FSC certification is widely used in printing, packaging, furniture, and construction industries.
  • The organization continues to strengthen policies on deforestation, climate action, and sustainable supply chains.

MSME ZED

The MSME ZED (Zero Defect Zero Effect) Certification was introduced by the Ministry of Micro, Small & Medium Enterprises (MSME), Government of India to promote quality manufacturing with minimal environmental impact. The initiative aims to help MSMEs adopt global quality standards, improve efficiency, and ensure sustainability.

Present – Integration with Global Quality Standards

  • MSME ZED certification is now aligned with ISO, Six Sigma, TQM (Total Quality Management), and Lean Manufacturing principles.
  • Many Indian and multinational companies require ZED certification for vendor approval and supplier partnerships.

GHG

The concept of GHG (Greenhouse Gas) Assessment has evolved over the past few decades as global awareness of climate change, carbon emissions, and sustainability has increased. Governments, organizations, and industries worldwide have adopted GHG measurement and reporting as part of their commitment to reducing environmental impact.

2020s – Digital & AI-Based GHG Monitoring

  • Advancements in AI, IoT, and blockchain led to real-time carbon tracking and automated sustainability reporting.
  • Governments introduced carbon taxes and mandatory reporting laws (EU CBAM, SEC climate disclosures).
  • Organizations focused on carbon offset programs, renewable energy adoption, and supply chain sustainability.

GMI

Graphic Measures International (GMI) was founded to establish a standardized approach to print quality control and color management in the packaging and printing industry. The organization was created to help global brands maintain consistent, high-quality printing across different suppliers and production facilities worldwide.

Present – Industry Leadership in Print Quality Control

  • Today, GMI is a leading global print quality certification program, ensuring color accuracy, brand consistency, and supplier reliability for packaging manufacturers.
  • It continues to evolve with AI-driven print quality inspection, cloud-based reporting, and automated color correction technologies.

GMI certification became a requirement for suppliers of major brands such as Walmart, Target, CVS, Walgreens, and Home Depot.

GMI was acquired by SGSCO, a global leader in inspection, verification, testing, and certification.
